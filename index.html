Artificial Life and Digital Evolution

I developed a Digital Evolution environment to study the intricate relationship between host organisms and parasites. In this environment, blue organisms represent parasites, unable to reproduce independently, while orange organisms represent hosts that can reproduce either with other orange organisms or be exploited by parasites for reproduction. Each organism possesses a unique genome and evolves over time to enhance its efficiency in achieving its objectives, with evolutionary progress indicated by slight color changes. The results of my experiment revealed that the presence of parasites unexpectedly benefits host organisms by increasing competition.
